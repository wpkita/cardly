# Rummy 500 Rules

Rummy 500 is a popular variant of rummy. The distinctive feature of Rummy 500 is that each player scores the value of the sets or cards they meld. It may be played by 2 to 4 players.

## Cards and Dealing

Rummy 500 is played using a standard French deck of 52 cards. Jokers are not used.

The first dealer is randomly selected. Cards are dealt one at a time to each player face down, clockwise starting at the dealer's left. The number of cards dealt is always 7.

The remaining cards are placed in a single pile face down between the players, forming the stock. The top card of the stock is turned face up and placed besides the stock to start the discard pile. As play continues any cards added to the discard pile are placed face up on top of any cards already in the discard pile. The discard pile should be slightly spread, so that players can readily see all the cards in it. After a round is complete, the next player to the left becomes the dealer.

## Gameplay

The object of the game is to score points as in regular rummy by laying down or laying off cards, initially in groups of matching cards known as melds, with a meld consisting of either: 3 or 4 cards of the same rank (e.g. 8♠ 8♥ 8♣ or 8♦ 8♠ 8♥ 8♣) called a set; or in sequences of three or more cards of the same suit (e.g. 8♠ 9♠ 10♠) called a run.

Aces are always a high card (e.g. Q♠ K♠ A♠).

Each player in turn, beginning with the player to the left of the dealer, may draw either the top card of the stock or any card from the discard pile. Once a card is picked up, either from the stock or the discard pile, it is final and no other cards may be picked up. However, there are two conditions when drawing more than one card from the discard pile:

- The player must take all the cards on top of (i.e., discarded after) the selected card.
- The selected card so drawn must immediately be used, either by laying it down in a meld or by laying it off on any meld already on the table. If the selected card cannot immediately be used in this way, the draw is illegal and the cards must be returned to the discard pile.
  - NOTE: The one exception to this is that a player may pick up only the top card from the discard pile and keep it in their hand without immediately playing it.

When drawing a card from the discard pile, any remaining cards taken with it may be either melded in the same turn or simply added to the player's hand.

During a player's turn, after drawing but before discarding, they may lay down any meld of matching cards, or may lay off any cards that match a meld or cards that have already been played. A player may lay down a single card or more, but only if they match cards that have already been played. For example, a player may lay down a single card (e.g. 7♠) on either a set that has already been laid down (e.g. 7♦ 7♣ 7♥), or a run or part of a run that has already been laid down (e.g. 8♠ 9♠ 10♠ or 8♠ 9♠). A player may also lay down a pair of cards in sequence of the same suit on a run or part of a run that has already been laid down (e.g. lay down the 6♠ 7♠ on a run of 8♠ 9♠ 10♠ or 8♠ 9♠ previously laid down).

Cards that are laid down or laid off are kept spread out on the table in front of the player, visible to other players. The player ends their turn by discarding a single card from their hand to the discard pile. A player cannot end a round without physically placing a card onto the discard pile.

Players are only permitted to lay down cards when it is their turn, after drawing but before discarding.

The round is over when one player discards the final card from their hand, either by melding or laying off all cards. Players receive no bonus for finishing first.

## Scoring

Face cards count as 10 as does the 10 card. 2-9 are 5 points. The Ace is always 15 points.

There is no "entry" score minimum a player must reach before being on the scoreboard.

When any player discards the last card in their hand, the play immediately ends. Each player's score is then figured as follows: The player is credited with the point value of all cards that he has showing on the table. From this figure is subtracted the point value of all cards remaining in his hand. The difference is added or subtracted from their score, as the case may be. A player's cumulative score may go indefinitely negative.

For example, if the cards they have shown total 85 points, and the cards left in their hand total 90 points, 5 points are subtracted from their previous net score.

## Ending the Game

If the stock pile runs out of cards, play continues normally through the remainder of that turn. When the next discard is made next to an empty stock pile, that discard and all remaining discard pile cards are immediately shuffled into a new stock pile. The top card of the newly shuffled stock is then turned face up to begin a new discard pile, and play continues.

The first player whose score reaches +500 wins the game. If two or more players reach 500 on the same hand, the one who goes out is the winner.

### Floating

Any player must discard on the turn in which they go out. A player cannot end a round without physically placing a card onto the discard pile — a completed turn is not considered complete without a discard. So, if a player plays the last card from their hand on a meld and have no remaining cards to discard, they are known as "floating" and play continues to the next player.

For example, if a player held a hand of two 3's and picked up another 3, this player would be unable to end the game as they would not have a discard. They would lay down their set of 3's and play continues to the next player despite a card not having been discarded. On the player's subsequent turn (the one who played the 3's in this example), they may end the game *if* their next drawn card is not part of any meld and therefore must be discarded.
